## 🎯 FRAMEWORK 1: Jab Jab Jab Right Hook (Gary Vaynerchuk)

**Proporção Core:** 80% Jabs + 20% Right Hooks

### What's a JAB?
**Jab = Valor puro, sem pedir nada em troca**
- Educação (dica, framework, learning)
- Inspiração (mindset, filosofia, histórias)
- Entretenimento (humor, curiosidade, surpresa)
- Comunidade (perguntas, conversations, UGC)

**Goal:** Build trust, provide value, make audience love you

### What's a RIGHT HOOK?
**Right Hook = Pedir algo em troca**
- Venda direta (link, preço, compre agora)
- Lead magnet (email, lista, download)
- Action (comment, share, follow)

**Goal:** Convert trust into action/revenue

### The Ratio Logic
**For Every 5 Posts:**
- 4 posts = Jabs (puro value, educação, inspiração)
- 1 post = Right Hook (venda, lead gen, oferta)

**Why?** Trust-building antes de vender. Audience se sente utilisada se muito sell.

### If You Break The Ratio
- **Too many Jabs (90%+):** Audience loves you but never converts. Great engagement, zero revenue.
- **Too many Hooks (40%+):** Audience ghosta. High unfollow rate, baixo engagement, algoritmo demota.

**Implementation Rule:** 
- Review calendar monthly
- Count: # of value posts vs # of sell posts
- Adjust if not 80/20

## 📊 FRAMEWORK 2: Content Pillars (Brunson + Vaynerchuk)

**Pillar = Tema recorrente que você SEMPRE fala**

### Why Pillars Matter
- Público sabe o que esperar
- Você não fica perdido no que postar
- Algoritmo reconhece padrão (favors consistency)
- Fácil prever: "Qual será meu próximo tema?"

### Designing Your Pillars

**Step 1: Identify 3-4 Core Themes**
Baseado em:
- Seu expertise (o que você sabe bem?)
- Que problema você resolve?
- Que resultado você delivera?

**Step 2: Assign Percentage to Each**
- Pilar 1 (strongest): 35-45%
- Pilar 2 (secondary): 25-35%
- Pilar 3 (supportive): 15-25%
- Pilar 4 (optional/rare): 0-15%

**Step 3: Validate Sustainability**
- Can you create 2-3 posts/week in strongest pillar? YES = good
- No = pillar too narrow, split it

### Example Pillar Structure

| Pilar | % | What It Delivers | Example Post |
|-------|---|---|---|
| **Frameworks** | 40% | How-to, step-by-step learning | "5 Levels of Awareness in Copywriting" |
| **Social Proof** | 30% | Results, testimonials, case studies | "From 0 to $100k with direct response" |
| **Mindset** | 20% | Philosophy, beliefs, motivation | "Why most copywriters fail (and how to fix it)" |
| **Offers** | 10% | Product, course, services | "Join my course, link in bio" |

**Validation Checklist:**
- [ ] Each pilar can sustain 2-3 posts/week?
- [ ] Total = 100%?
- [ ] Each pilar has clear "what you deliver"?
- [ ] Mix feels natural (not forced)?

### Decision Tree: Which Pilar?

When creating new post:

```
1. Topic comes up — what pilar fits?
   ├─ Educational? → Frameworks pilar
   ├─ Proof-based? → Social Proof pilar
   ├─ Mindset/Philosophy? → Mindset pilar
   └─ Selling something? → Offers pilar

2. Count posts in that pilar this week
   ├─ Already hit quota? → Switch to low pilar
   └─ Room for more? → Go ahead

3. Check 80/20 rule
   ├─ Total Jabs + Hooks balanced? YES → publish
   └─ Too many Hooks? → Use Jab instead
```

## 📅 FRAMEWORK 3: Soap Opera Sequence (Russell Brunson)

**Sequence = Multi-part narrative that unfolds over days/weeks**

### Why Sequences Matter
- Hooks audience DAILY (they wait for next episode)
- Makes memorability (story > standalone facts)
- Builds momentum toward offer/point
- Increases engagement (comments like "when's part 2?")

### Classic 5-Day Sequence

**Day 1: The Hook**
> "I just realized something that changes everything..."

**Goal:** Create curiosity gap. Make them NEED to follow for Day 2.

**Day 2: The Problem**
> "Here's what I discovered... [problem statement]"

**Goal:** Validate that audience has this problem too. Build desire to solve it.

**Day 3: The Agitation** 
> "Most people try X, but here's why it fails..."

**Goal:** Show why normal solutions don't work. Make audience feel stuck.

**Day 4: The Solution**
> "What actually works is [method/framework]"

**Goal:** Reveal solution. Build hope that it's possible.

**Day 5: The Offer**
> "Here's exactly how to get [result]..."

**Goal:** Sell the course, lead magnet, service. Close the loop.

### Implementation: Sequence vs Standalone

**When to Sequence:**
- Major topic (product launch, big announcement)
- Educational series (5-step framework)
- Case study (journey story)
- Controversial take (build argument over multiple posts)

**When to Standalone:**
- Trends (timely, must post now)
- Micro-tips (can stand alone)
- Community posts (don't need narrative)
- Quick wins (5-minute hacks)

### Calendar Trick: Map Sequences in Advance

```
Week 1, Mon-Fri: Sequence A (5 parts)
Week 2, Mon: Standalone post
Week 2, Tue-Fri: Sequence B (4 parts)
Week 3: All standalone (community, trending)
Week 4, Mon-Fri: Sequence C (5 parts)
```

This ensures sequences don't cluster, gives audience variety.

## ⏰ FRAMEWORK 4: Frequency & Timing (Alex Hormozi)

### The Consistency Principle

**Consistency > Frequency**

- **Bad:** 10x/week for 2 weeks, then disappear for a month
- **Good:** 3x/week FOREVER (even if 1 post quality isn't perfect)

Why? Algorithm notices pattern. Audience expects consistency. You build habit.

### Frequency Levels

**Beginner (1-2 posts/week):**
- Sustainable if full-time job
- Instagram still notices
- Long-term win

**Intermediate (3-4 posts/week):**
- Realistic for content team
- Algorithm LOVES (consistency signal)
- Recommended minimum

**Advanced (5-7 posts/week):**
- Requires production machine
- Diminishing returns after 5/week
- Only if quality stays high

### Timing Logic (When to Post)

**What to Optimize For:**
- Reach (how many see it?)
- Engagement (saves, comments, clicks)
- Retention (do they follow after?)

**Best Times (Instagram 2025):**
- **Peak Engagement:** 9-10 AM, 6-7 PM (general)
- **Niche-Dependent:** Educational content peaks midday, entertainment peaks evening
- **Key:** Post when YOUR audience is active (use Instagram Insights)

### Decision Tree: Posting Time

```
1. Check your Instagram Insights
   ├─ "Best times to post" = [X]
   └─ Your audience is most active [day/time]

2. Can you post then?
   ├─ YES → use that time
   └─ NO → pick closest time

3. Test: Change posting time every week
   ├─ Week 1: 9 AM
   ├─ Week 2: 12 PM
   ├─ Week 3: 6 PM
   └─ Track reach, pick winner

4. Lock it in: Post same time daily/weekly
```

## 📋 FRAMEWORK 5: The Format Mix (Vaynerchuk)

**Format = How content is delivered (reel, carousel, post)**

### Instagram 2025 Algorithm Priority
1. **Reels** (highest reach, 3-5x more than carousel)
   - Short-form video (15-90 seconds)
   - Need strong hook in first 1-2 seconds
   - Saves/rewatches = algorithm boost

2. **Carousels** (moderate reach, good for education)
   - 5-10 slides per carousel
   - Saves rate high (people bookmark)
   - Good for step-by-step, sequential learning

3. **Posts** (low reach, intimate audience)
   - Static image + caption
   - Good for personal, conversational
   - Better for engagement depth (comments)

### Recommended Mix

| Format | % | Why |
|--------|---|-----|
| Reels | 50-60% | Algorithm prioritizes, highest reach |
| Carousels | 30-40% | High save rate, educational content |
| Posts | 5-10% | Personal connection, lower reach OK |

### Decision Tree: What Format?

```
Content Type → Best Format

"How-to / Step-by-step"
   ├─ 5+ steps? → Carousel
   └─ <3 steps? → Reel with text overlay

"Before/After / Transformation"
   → Reel (visual impact) or Post (personal)

"Trending topic / Hook-heavy"
   → Reel (hook in first 2 sec critical)

"Social proof / Testimonial"
   → Reel (emotional, visual) or Carousel (detailed)

"Question / Engagement bait"
   → Post (encourages comments)

"Controversy / Take"
   → Post (allows longer caption, deeper comments)
```

## 🔄 WORKFLOW: Creating Your Monthly Calendar

### Step 1: Count Available Posts (2 hours)

**Input:** Frequency goal (ex: 4 posts/week)

```
4 posts/week × 4 weeks = 16 posts/month
```

### Step 2: Distribute by Pilar (1 hour)

**Input:** Your 3 pillars + %

```
Pillar 1 (40%) = 16 × 0.40 = 6-7 posts
Pillar 2 (35%) = 16 × 0.35 = 5-6 posts
Pillar 3 (25%) = 16 × 0.25 = 4 posts
```

### Step 3: Apply 80/20 Jab/Hook (1 hour)

```
16 posts × 80% = 13 Jabs
16 posts × 20% = 3 Right Hooks

Spread hooks across month (not all Week 1!)
```

### Step 4: Assign Formats (1 hour)

```
16 posts, 50% reels = 8 reels
16 posts, 35% carousel = 6 carousels
16 posts, 15% posts = 2 posts
```

### Step 5: Map Sequences (1-2 hours)

```
Weeks 1-2: One 5-part sequence
Week 3: Standalone (trending, community)
Week 4: One 4-part sequence
```

### Step 6: Assign Timing (1 hour)

```
Every Monday 9 AM
Every Wednesday 6 PM
Every Friday 10 AM
Every Sunday 7 PM (if 4/week)
```

### Step 7: Validate & Publish Calendar

- [ ] Total posts = goal?
- [ ] Pillar distribution ±10%?
- [ ] 80/20 ratio respected?
- [ ] Format mix diverse?
- [ ] Sequences spaced out?
- [ ] Timing realistic?

## ✅ CHECKLIST: Before Publishing Calendar

- [ ] **Frequency sustainable?** Can you create X posts/week EVERY week for 3+ months?
- [ ] **Pillar % correct?** Each pilar has minimum 2-3 posts to establish pattern
- [ ] **Mix of formats?** Reels + carousels + posts (not all reels)
- [ ] **Timing optimized?** Post when audience is active (per Insights)
- [ ] **Sequences planned?** 5-day narratives spaced throughout month
- [ ] **Jab/Hook ratio?** 80/20 respected, hooks spread out
- [ ] **Content briefs ready?** Each post has theme, hook type, pillar, CTA clear
- [ ] **Buffer built?** 2-3 posts ahead of schedule in case life happens

## 🚨 Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Mistake 1: All reels, no carousels/posts**
- Reels are exhausting to make
- Variety keeps audience engaged
- Mix it: 60% reels, 30% carousel, 10% posts

**Mistake 2: Sequence overuse**
- Sequences are powerful BUT audiences get tired
- Use 1-2/month max, not every week

**Mistake 3: Inconsistent posting time**
- "I'll post when I feel like it"
- Algorithm punishes inconsistency
- Pick time, stick with it

**Mistake 4: Forgetting 80/20**
- 5 posts selling, 1 value = unfollow city
- Keep ratio in check monthly

**Mistake 5: Overloaded pillars**
- 6+ pillars = confusing, hard to maintain
- Stick to 3-4, clear, repeatable themes

## 🎯 Your Decision Framework

**As Content Planner, ask yourself:**

1. **Frequency:** How many posts is realistic/sustainable?
2. **Pillars:** What 3 themes will I repeat forever?
3. **Format:** What's my realistic format mix?
4. **Jab/Hook:** Am I balancing value + sales?
5. **Timing:** When does audience show up?
6. **Sequences:** Which topics deserve multi-part stories?

Answer these, your calendar writes itself.
